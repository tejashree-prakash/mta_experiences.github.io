Data Information

About Our Data

Our analysis is based on NYC Open Data’s NYC Open Data’s 2019 Subway Rider Survey , a public dataset that captures how New Yorkers feel about subway service, delays, affordability, and overall satisfaction.

In addition to the survey data, we incorporate:

  • Official subway line colors from NYC Open Data’s MTA colors dataset (2025) to match line families to their familiar hex color codes in our tables and visualizations.
  • Subway route geometry from the NYU Spatial Data Repository (2016 NYC Subway Routes) to recreate an interactive subway map for exploring composite scores and rankings by line family.

Data Dictionary

Our cleaned dataset rider_data_full contains 5,417 rows and 19 main variables:

  • survey_id: Unique numeric identifier for each survey response.
  • subway_line_used_most_often: Subway line the respondent reports using most often.
  • use_of_subway_frequency: How frequently the respondent uses the subway (e.g., every day, several times a week).
  • get_to_subway_via: Primary way the respondent travels to the subway (e.g., walking, bus, bike).
  • primary_use_of_subway: Main reason for using the subway (e.g., commuting to work or school, leisure, errands).
  • average_length_subway_ride: Typical duration of a subway ride for the respondent.
  • overall_satisfaction: Self-reported satisfaction with subway service (e.g., satisfied, neutral, unsatisfied).
  • frequency_of_delays: How often the respondent experiences subway delays.
  • approximate_delay_duration: Typical length of delays when they occur (e.g., under 10 minutes, 10–20 minutes, more than 20 minutes).
  • alternative_transport: What the respondent is likely to do if a delay greater than 15 minutes occurs while waiting for a train (e.g., wait, take another route, use rideshare or taxi).
  • frequency_of_rerouting: How often the respondent experiences service changes or rerouting (planned or unplanned).
  • top_three_complaints: Text field summarizing the respondent’s main concerns or complaints about transit service.
  • most_common_reason_for_delay: The most common reason given for delays (e.g., signal problems, train traffic, mechanical issues), as reported by the respondent.
  • is_subway_affordable: Respondent’s perception of whether subway fares are affordable.
  • opinion_on_increased_fares: Respondent’s opinion about proposed MTA fare increases (e.g., support, oppose, neutral).
  • submitted_at: Date and time when the survey response was submitted.
  • survey_stop_borough: Borough where the survey was conducted for in-person responses (retained in some intermediate datasets but dropped from the final analysis dataset).
  • survey_stop_location: Station where the survey was filled in for in-person responses (also removed from the final analysis dataset).
  • zip_code: Respondent’s ZIP code, used to derive borough and explore spatial patterns.
  • collection_method: How the response was collected (e.g., online or in-person).
  • borough: Borough associated with the respondent’s ZIP code (including some ZIP codes outside the five core NYC boroughs, labeled accordingly).
